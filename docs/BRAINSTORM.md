# Geopolitics Economy Game – Core Concept (Brainstorm)

**Pitch:** Knights-and-Merchants-style economy and building, but countries are the players and the setting is modern global geopolitics. All visuals are programmatic (no textures): shapes, colors, lines, and simple animations only.

## Inspiration from Knights and Merchants (adapted)

- **Multi-step production chains** – e.g. Oil → Refinery → Fuel/Plastics; Ore → Steel Mill → Arms/Infrastructure; Grain → Food Processing → Rations/Export.
- **Connectivity matters** – "roads" become trade routes, pipelines, supply chains; placement and links affect efficiency and vulnerability.
- **Population/food analogue** – citizens (or "stability/living standards") consume goods; shortages hurt growth and military readiness.
- **No hard unit cap** – scaling limited by economy, map, and balance levers (e.g. upkeep, stability).
- **Tech via building** – constructing certain buildings unlocks new chains, units, or policies.

## Modern geopolitics layer

- **Countries as players** – each player controls a nation (or bloc). Map = world or region; territories, borders, capitals.
- **Resources** – energy (oil, gas, nuclear, renewables), minerals, food, industrial capacity, "influence" or "soft power" as a resource.
- **Buildings** – refineries, factories, research centers, military bases, ports, trade hubs, dams, etc.
- **Improvement** – upgrade buildings, invest in regions, research techs that improve efficiency or unlock new chains.
- **Balance levers** – upkeep costs, diminishing returns on same-type buildings, bottlenecks (e.g. rare minerals), events (sanctions, crises) to keep economy and military in tension.

## Procedural "no textures" art direction

- Terrain: colored regions (e.g. elevation/biome by heightmap or noise), borders as lines.
- Buildings: simple shapes (rectangles, circles) with size/color indicating type/level; maybe icons as drawn shapes (e.g. factory = gear outline).
- Units/armies: dots, triangles, or small formations; color = owner, size = strength.
- UI: panels, bars, and icons as vector-style rectangles, lines, and primitive shapes.
- Optional: subtle animation (pulsing, movement along routes) to keep the world readable and alive.

## Scope guardrails for "balanced"

- Clear **win/end conditions** (e.g. economic victory, territorial control, or scenario objectives).
- **Meaningful choices** – e.g. invest in military vs. economy vs. stability; short-term gains vs. long-term tech.
- **Counterplay** – no single dominant strategy; trade-offs between expansion, defense, and internal development.
